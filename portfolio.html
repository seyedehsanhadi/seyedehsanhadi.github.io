Nanocellulose-Reinforced Composites

Integrating nanocellulose fibers into polymer matrices for improved tensile strength and reduced material weight. This approach harnesses renewable resources for sustainable yet robust composite structures.

Key Highlights

  • 30% boost in tensile strength vs. conventional composites.
  • Notable density reduction for lightweight components ideal for automotive applications.
  • Findings published in Composites Science & Technology.

Magnetic Hybrid Foams

Engineered foams infused with magnetic particles to enable unique self-assembling and sensing functionalities. Ideal for aerospace insulation, environmental remediation, and advanced fluidics.

Key Highlights

  • 25% enhanced thermal insulation compared to conventional polyurethane foams.
  • Potential for remote manipulation and shape correction under external magnetic fields.
  • Collaboration with industry partners to scale up production.

Sustainable Fiber Packaging

Biodegradable packaging materials constructed from natural fibers like hemp and jute. Aiming to reduce petrochemical plastic usage and advance eco-friendly consumer solutions.

Key Highlights

  • Created packaging prototypes with complete biodegradability.
  • Achieved comparable strength and flexibility to traditional plastic packaging.
  • Received a grant from the Environmental Sustainability Council.

High-Performance CNT Composites

Investigating the incorporation of carbon nanotubes (CNTs) into polymer matrices to significantly improve electrical conductivity and mechanical strength. This project opens avenues for advanced applications in electronics and aerospace industries.

Key Highlights

  • Enhanced electrical conductivity by 50% with minimal CNT addition.
  • Improved mechanical properties, achieving a 40% increase in modulus.
  • Presented findings at the International Conference on Nanotechnology.
