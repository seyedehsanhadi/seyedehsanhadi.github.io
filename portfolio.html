Nanocellulose-Reinforced Polymer Composites

This project focuses on the integration of nanocellulose fibers into polymer matrices to create composites with superior mechanical properties. The use of nanocellulose not only enhances strength and durability but also contributes to the sustainability of the materials by utilizing renewable resources.

Key Achievements:

  • Achieved a 30% increase in tensile strength compared to traditional composites.
  • Reduced composite density by 15%, resulting in lightweight materials suitable for automotive applications.
  • Published findings in the Journal of Composite Materials.

View Publication

Magnetic Hybrid Foams

Developed hybrid foams embedded with magnetic particles, enabling applications in smart materials and environmental remediation. These foams exhibit enhanced thermal insulation and can be manipulated using external magnetic fields.

Key Achievements:

  • Introduced magnetic responsiveness to lightweight foam structures.
  • Enhanced thermal insulation properties by 25% compared to conventional foams.
  • Collaborated with industry partners for real-world application testing.

View Publication

Fiber-Based Sustainable Packaging

Designed and developed sustainable packaging materials using natural fibers, aiming to reduce reliance on fossil-based plastics. The project emphasizes biodegradability and recyclability without compromising on performance.

Key Achievements:

  • Created packaging prototypes with complete biodegradability.
  • Achieved comparable strength and flexibility to traditional plastic packaging.
  • Received a grant from the Environmental Sustainability Council.

High-Performance Carbon Nanotube Composites

Investigated the incorporation of carbon nanotubes (CNTs) into polymer matrices to significantly improve electrical conductivity and mechanical strength. This project opens avenues for advanced applications in electronics and aerospace industries.

Key Achievements:

  • Enhanced electrical conductivity by 50% with minimal CNT addition.
  • Improved mechanical properties, achieving a 40% increase in modulus.
  • Presented findings at the International Conference on Nanotechnology.
